About:

I am committed to data-driven urban planning, using data analytics to address the major issues that cities continue to face, and digging into the data to find answers to questions from an urban planner's perspective.

Research Interests

My research interest lies in the evolving field of data-driven urban development, where the integration of innovative tools and technologies is reshaping the dynamics of city planning and management. Focusing on the application of big data, my work aims to transform how we understand and design urban spaces. I am particularly interested in the collection, analysis, and visualization of diverse city-related data, ranging from environmental indicators to social and economic patterns. This approach helps in uncovering insights into urban issues and lifestyles, providing a comprehensive understanding of the complexities of modern cities.

The core of my research is to leverage these insights to inform urban policy and improve the quality of life in urban areas. By identifying trends and forecasting urban needs, my goal is to contribute to creating more efficient, sustainable, and inclusive cities. This involves not only understanding the intricate dynamics of urban environments but also actively participating in their evolution, addressing challenges such as optimizing public resources, enhancing environmental sustainability, and fostering community well-being. In essence, my research is dedicated to utilizing data as a tool to make cities better places to live for current and future generations.
